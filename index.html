VI. “Jesus walked in Galilee… because the Jews sought to kill Him.” (John 7:1)

(Special training for the Twelve: Out of favor and on the move in and around Galilee)
NOTE: The change in the focus of Jesus’ ministry has become complete by this point. He has turned from public proclamation and presentation of Himself as the Messiah of Israel to private preparation of His disciples for His death and absence; from miracles – the chief validating sign of His Messianic identity – to parables, a teaching method marvelously suited to instruct His followers while not engaging His enemies; from a ministry focused on all Israel to one intended only for His followers. The Jews’ hatred of Jesus is growing intense; He knows His time is short, and He sets out to ready His disciples for His death and the responsibilities which would be theirs at that time.

VII. “They sought therefore to take Him, but no man laid hands on Him, because His hour was not yet come.” (John 7:30)

(Preaching the Gospel and training the Twelve; the last six months before the Lord’s Passion, in Judea, Jerusalem, and Trans-Jordan)
NOTE: The events of this period, the last six months of Jesus’ public ministry, are best understood in relation to three trips to Jerusalem (or its environs) made by Jesus during this time. We must piece together all four Gospels to understand the chronology of this period, simply because no one Gospel provides a continuous narrative. The three trips and the chronological scheme which may be developed around them are as follows:
1. To Jerusalem, for the Feast of Tabernacles (early October) – Luke 9:51 = departure for Jerusalem; John 7:2 – 10:21 narrates events of this trip.
2. To Jerusalem, for the Feast of Dedication (late December) – Luke 13:22 speaks of Jesus’ journey toward Jerusalem; John 10:22-39 describes the events of this trip. After this trip, because of the hostility in Jerusalem, Jesus fled to Trans-Jordan (John 10:40-41), where He ministered for a time.
3. To Bethany (two miles from Jerusalem) – John 11:1-54 describes the raising of Lazarus, while Luke 17:11 speaks of Jesus’ journey to Jerusalem at this time. After this trip to Bethany, Jesus finds seclusion in Ephraim (John 11:54), a little village north of Jerusalem some miles. Here He remains with His disciples, away from His enemies, until He goes for the final time to Jerusalem, during the Passover season.

UNIT THREE: “…again, I leave the world…” (John 16:28c)

I. “Behold, we go up to Jerusalem, and the Son of man shall be delivered… and they shall condemn Him to death…” (Mark 10:33) (The final journey to Jerusalem)

NOTE: Jesus had been residing for a few quiet days at the small town of Ephraim, awaiting the time when “all things should be fulfilled.” Now He knows that that time is at hand, that the “Jews’ Passover was nigh,”, and that He must go with His disciples to Jerusalem, where He would “finish the work of Him that sent Me.” The Lord takes a rather lengthy route, traveling first to Perea, then crossing back into Judea at Jordan, moving down to Bethany, and then finally arriving in Jerusalem.
